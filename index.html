FastSwap: A Lightweight One-Stage Framework for Fast and Realistic Face Swapping


Anonymous Authors





Abstract

We propose FastSwap, a novel one-stage framework for fast face swapping, which aims to rapidly generate a high-fidelity face image with the identity of the source image and the pose and attributes of the target image. Unlike previous face swapping works, we design a shallow and lightweight network trained in a self-supervised manner without any manual annotations. The core of our framework is a novel decoder block, called Triple Adaptive Normalization (TAN) block, which effectively integrates the identity and pose information. Besides, we propose a novel data augmentation and switch-test strategy to extract the attributes from the target image, and such a strategy further enables controllable attribute editing. Extensive experiments on VoxCeleb2 and wild faces demonstrate that our framework generates high-fidelity face swapping results faster and better preserves the identity, pose, and attributes than other state-of-the-art methods. Furthermore, we conduct an in-depth study to understand the effect of each design component of our proposal.
